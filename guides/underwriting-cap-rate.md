# Underwriting a Bali Boutique Resort: A Conservative Cap-Rate Case Study (Ubud, 7 Villas)

Most Bali yield numbers online are fantasy — 80%+ occupancy assumed, gross revenue quoted as profit, costs ignored. Here is a transparent, conservative worked example, with an [interactive model](https://santarabali.com/finmodel) you can change yourself.

The asset: a seven-villa boutique resort in Desa Singakerta, Ubud (Gianyar), Bali. Four villas operate today (live on Booking.com); three are built to shell. Offered as a leasehold assignment, owner-direct.

## The numbers, stated conservatively
- **Phase 1** — 4 operating villas. Ask **USD 780,000**.
- **Phase 2** — 3 shell villas, ~**USD 100,000** finishing, ~3 months to operating. Ask **USD 390,000**.
- **Whole complex** — **USD 1,180,000**.

The anchor is contracted, not hoped-for: a **NOI floor of USD 84,000/year** on Phase 1 (USD 8,500/mo gross − USD 1,500/mo OpEx). Against the USD 780,000 ask, that is an approximate **10.8% cap-rate floor — before any upside**.

Defaults are deliberately cautious: **65% occupancy**, **USD 140 / USD 95** nightly rates, **33% OpEx**. The three assumptions are unpacked in [Three numbers that decide a villa's yield](three-numbers-yield.md).

## The value-add layer
Phase 2: three villas already have structure, walls and roofs. ~USD 100,000 of finishing and ~3 months takes the resort from four keys to seven, against ~USD 580,000 finished comparable value — a below-replacement-cost play, not passive yield. See [Operating vs off-plan](operating-vs-offplan.md).

## What to verify
Tenure (leasehold/akta sewa), the fixed-price extension, licences, Booking continuity — all in the [due-diligence checklist](due-diligence-checklist.md).
