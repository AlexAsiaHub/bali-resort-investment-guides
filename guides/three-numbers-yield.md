# The Three Numbers That Decide a Bali Villa's Yield: Occupancy, ADR and OpEx

Every Bali villa yield claim comes down to three assumptions. Get them honest and the cap rate is real; inflate any one and it is fiction. Test them live in the [interactive model](https://santarabali.com/finmodel).

## 1. Occupancy — 65%, not 85%
Listings quote peak-season 75–85% as if it were the annual average. A well-managed Ubud villa runs nearer **65%** across the year. Raise it only if you can prove year-round demand.

## 2. ADR — below benchmark on purpose
The base case uses **USD 140** for the 150 m² master and **USD 95** for the 100 m² townhouses — under typical Ubud benchmarks, so the floor case does not lean on premium pricing.

## 3. OpEx — the line listings delete
Many quote gross revenue and skip costs. A realistic operation runs ~**33% OpEx** (commissions, taxes, staff, utilities). The result: a contracted **NOI floor of USD 84,000/year** on the USD 780,000 Phase 1 ask — about a **10.8% cap-rate floor**.

How that floor is defended: [the conservative cap-rate case study](underwriting-cap-rate.md). What a full year of operating taught us about these inputs: [12 months on Booking.com](booking-com-lessons.md).
