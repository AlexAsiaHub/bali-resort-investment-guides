# A Due-Diligence Checklist for Buying a Leasehold Resort in Bali

You found a resort that operates and the cap rate looks real — that's half the work. Here's the rest.

## 1. Tenure and the extension clause
Leasehold (assignment of *akta sewa*), ~27 years remaining. Critical: the **extension terms**. Most Bali leases re-price at market; a **fixed-price** extension in the deed is rare and valuable. Verify wording and enforceability with Indonesian counsel.

## 2. Licences and classification
- **SLF** (building worthiness) and **NIB** (business ID) issued?
- **KBLI:** Pondok Wisata vs Hotel Melati — matches operation?
- **Gianyar zoning** (Yellow Zone / RTRW) — tourism accommodation permitted?

## 3. The Booking.com question
Operating history proves the business, but the merchant account is **not** transferable real property — plan a credentials/playbook handover.

## 4. Ownership structure
Foreigners can't hold freehold; close in personal name or your own Indonesian PT; model PPh/BPHTB tax into real entry cost.
